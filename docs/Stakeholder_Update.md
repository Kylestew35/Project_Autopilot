Stakeholder Update: Careful Cravings Project

Project Overview
The Careful Cravings project is an AI-powered healthy food recommendation engine that aims to help users make healthier eating choices and reduce the risk of chronic diseases. The project is currently in the Design phase, with the goal of defining the product architecture, user experience, and technical requirements.

Key Highlights
- Discovery phase completed, including market research, user interviews, and competitive analysis. Insights gathered will inform the product design.
- Product design and technical design workstreams are underway, focusing on the user interface, information architecture, and backend systems.
- AI model development has commenced, with the goal of designing and training the recommendation engine.
- Automation workflows have been implemented, including Jira issue tracking, GitHub commit monitoring, Slack user feedback, and Notion task synchronization.

Objectives and Metrics
- Achieve a user acquisition target of 50,000 registered users within the first 6 months of launch.
- Maintain a user retention rate of at least 75% over the first 12 months of operation.
- Increase the average user engagement time by 30% within the first 6 months of launch.
- Achieve a customer satisfaction score of at least 4.5 out of 5 within the first 12 months of operation.

Risks and Mitigation Strategies
- Regulatory Compliance: Closely monitor and adhere to relevant food and health regulations.
- Data Privacy and Security: Implement robust data privacy and security measures to protect user information.
- AI Model Accuracy: Continuously refine and optimize the AI recommendation engine to improve accuracy.
- User Adoption: Develop a comprehensive marketing and user engagement strategy to drive adoption.

Next Steps
- Complete the product and technical design workstreams, including the AI model development.
- Commence the development phase, focusing on the frontend, backend, and AI integration.
- Prepare for the testing phase, including functional, performance, and user acceptance testing.
